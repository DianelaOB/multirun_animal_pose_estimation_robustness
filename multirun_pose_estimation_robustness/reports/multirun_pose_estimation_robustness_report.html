Animal Pose Estimation: Multi-Run Robustness Study

Executive Summary

  • Across 5 independently trained models, baseline mean localization error was 2.82 ± 1.96 pixels, and baseline PCK@5 was 87.3 ± 14.9%.
  • Mild Gaussian Blur produced a mean paired change of +0.95 ± 0.54 pixels and increased error in every run (5/5).
  • Strong Gaussian Blur produced a mean paired change of +7.07 ± 1.07 pixels and increased error in every run (5/5).
  • Gaussian Noise produced a mean paired change of +31.97 ± 32.58 pixels and increased error in every run (5/5).
  • Brightness produced a mean paired change of +0.97 ± 0.92 pixels and increased error in most runs (4/5).
  • Contrast produced a mean paired change of +0.11 ± 0.15 pixels and increased error in most runs (4/5).
  • Central Occlusion produced a mean paired change of +47.16 ± 6.91 pixels and increased error in every run (5/5).
  • The largest average degradation was observed for Central Occlusion (+47.16 pixels relative to baseline).
  • Across runs, the highest baseline landmark error was observed for leg_fr_tip (6.98 px), while abdomen had the lowest (0.77 px).

Experimental Design

5 independently initialized U-Net models were evaluated on a fixed validation split. Every perturbation was compared with the same model's baseline.

Limitations

  • The study includes 5 independent runs; more runs would provide a more precise estimate of variability.
  • Every model was trained for 3 epochs using the original course-scale architecture and training settings.
  • Only one predefined severity was used for noise, brightness, contrast, and occlusion, while two blur severities were tested.
  • The validation split was fixed to isolate training variability; results may also vary under different data splits.
  • The paired statistical tests are exploratory and have limited power with a small number of runs.
  • The study evaluates robustness to synthetic perturbations and does not establish performance under all real-world imaging conditions.
